WHEN SIR RATAN TATA JOINED TATA GROUP

Ratan Tata became the chairman of Tata Sons in 1991, when India's economy opened up to foreign competition. During this time, Tata's leadership transformed the Tata Group through restructuring, acquisitions, and international expansion

Acquisitions
Tata led the group in acquiring several companies, including Tetley, Corus, Jaguar Land Rover, and Daewoo. These acquisitions helped the Tata Group become a prominent player in international markets
Restructuring
Tata steered the group through the economic reforms of 1991, setting the stage for the group's globalization
International expansion
The Tata Group's revenues increased more than 40 times under Tata's leadership, reaching $103 billion by 2016
IPO
Tata oversaw the initial public offering (IPO) of TCS in 2004, which was India's largest and Asia's second-largest IPO at the time.
Nano
Tata's company Tata Motors launched the Tata Nano, which was presented as "the world's most affordable car" in 2008.
Tata was born in 1937 in Bombay, British India (present-day Mumbai). He studied at Cornell University and Harvard Business School.
